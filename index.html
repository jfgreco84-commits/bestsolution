Easy Two-Step Process

Two products, endless shine. Restore the metal, then bring the shine.

Step 1 · C5 Restoration Lotion

Shake & Rub

Shake it well first. C5 settles like orange juice (it never dries out or goes bad).

Dab a little on a paper towel, old t-shirt, or microfiber cloth and rub the metal hard. It removes heavy tarnish, oxidation, cloudiness, and dullness from metal. The shine is back in seconds.

Step 2 · Best Solution Polish

Spray, Brush, Wipe, or Soak

Soak dirty pieces a few hours or overnight, then rinse. (Never soak old opals or pearls.)

Or spray it on, get it wet, and scrub with a soft toothbrush, getting behind the stones. Safe on every stone, no ammonia or acid. Rinse and done, brilliance restored.

What is C5?
C5 is our fine metal restoration lotion, the deep-restore partner to your polish. Where Best Solution polish brings back everyday sparkle, C5 goes after the tough stuff: heavy tarnish, oxidation, cloudiness, and dullness on gold, silver, platinum, palladium, brass, copper, chrome, stainless steel, pewter, and nickel. It isn't a cloth or a wipe. It's a rich lotion you work into the metal by hand or with a cloth until the original luster comes back. Use the polish for the overall shine and reach for C5 on the spots polish alone can't bring back.
What can Best Solution restore?
Just about anything with shine. Jewelry of every kind (rings, necklaces, bracelets, earrings, watches, pendants, charms), all types of stones, from hard gems like diamonds, rubies, emeralds, and sapphires to delicate opals & pearls, plus eyewear, coins & collectibles, home & kitchen (faucets, sinks, silverware, serving trays, tea sets, candlesticks), glass & mirrors, and auto & hobby (chrome, rims, motorcycle parts, tools). Works beautifully on both fine and costume jewelry. And because there's no acid, no ammonia, and no abrasives, it cleans without scratching, etching, or harming your pieces.

Why is it safe for opals & pearls?
Most Asked
Opals and pearls are soft, porous gems. Unlike a diamond, they actually hold natural water inside them and have a delicate surface that harsh chemicals can ruin permanently. Most jewelry cleaners rely on acid or ammonia, and those strip the moisture right out of these stones, leaving opals cracked, crazed, or cloudy and pearls dull, pitted, or discolored. That damage doesn't come back.

Best Solution has no acid, no ammonia, and no abrasives, so there is simply nothing in it to dry out, etch, or eat away at a porous stone. It gently lifts away dirt and oils and restores the shine the way fragile gems actually need. That's why it's safe to use on opals, pearls, turquoise, coral, amber, and other soft or porous stones that you'd never dare put a typical cleaner near.
Will it keep my white gold from turning yellow?
Here's something most people don't realize: white gold is really yellow gold. It just gets its bright, silvery look from a thin plating of rhodium. That rhodium layer is the only thing making it "white." As the piece is worn, a film of oxidation, oils, and tarnish builds up and that finish goes dull, letting the warm yellow gold underneath start to show through.

C5 is built to lift that oxidation and buildup right off. Clean your white gold with C5 regularly and you keep the finish bright and white instead of yellow, so your ring keeps looking like the day you got it, far longer. And with no acid or ammonia, it does it gently, without attacking the rhodium plating.
What should I wipe it on with?
A little tip from us: we use Bounty brand paper towels because they're actually made from a cotton fiber instead of the cheap paper fiber. That cotton is what helps pull the oxidation and tarnish off the metal. You don't have to use Bounty though. An old t-shirt, a microfiber cloth, or any soft cotton rag works great too. And a Q-tip is perfect for getting into hard-to-reach spots and intricate, detailed pieces.
